# Synopsis Packaging Guide

## Purpose

Use this guide when generating packaging-layer outputs such as:

- title directions
- synopsis variants
- opening-hook packaging

Do not use it as a substitute for actual chapter writing.

## Shared packaging principles

- start with protagonist + current trap or opportunity
- surface the shortest visible conflict
- hint at the strongest payoff direction
- end on an unresolved pull, not a flat summary
- avoid generic empty hype

## Platform guidance

### 番茄

- hook early
- keep conflict direct
- show short-term payoff visibility
- prefer fast, punchy wording

### 七猫

- make爽点 more explicit
- strengthen protagonist decisiveness
- keep stakes visible and practical

### 起点

- clarify premise and world hook quickly
- give the protagonist a stronger orientation
- avoid packaging that feels too hollow or too exaggerated

### 晋江

- prioritize emotional precision
- keep tone cleaner and more character-led
- avoid over-hard-sell packaging

## Title guidance

- prefer 3-7 Chinese characters for direct commercial styles when appropriate
- avoid titles that sound detached from the actual premise
- avoid overused generic power-fantasy combinations unless the project clearly calls for them

## Synopsis guidance

Good synopsis shape:

1. protagonist position
2. trigger or core change
3. strongest visible conflict
4. unresolved future pull

Avoid:

- full backstory dump
- worldbuilding lecture
- vague emotional filler
- repeating the title instead of adding information

## Opening-hook packaging guidance

Good opening-hook packaging should:

- say what pressure the protagonist is under now
- say what advantage or abnormality appears now
- preserve one unanswered question

It should not:

- explain the whole world
- resolve the main mystery too early
- sound like generic ad copy with no story signal
